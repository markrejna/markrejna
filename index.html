About Me

I am a student-athlete at the University of California, Berkeley, pursuing a Bachelor of Science in Chemical Engineering with a concentration in Biotechnology and a minor in Data Science. I was born in London, United Kingdom, and later moved to Prague, Czech Republic. I have embraced the challenges of adapting to new academic and cultural environments, both in my studies and as a member of the Cal Ice Hockey Team. Balancing athletics and academics has taught me useful time management, teamwork, hard work, and diligence skills. My academic interests are centered around the intersection of engineering and innovation.

Currently, I work as an Undergraduate Researcher in the Merchant Lab at UC Berkeley and as a Research Assistant at the Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory's Molecular Foundry. My research focuses on algal molecular biology and metal homeostasis, where I use systems biology, bioinformatics, and AI-assisted computational methods to investigate copper-associated regulatory pathways in photosynthetic algae. I also maintain a strong interest in neurodegenerative disease research.

Research Overview

Active Research Areas

Algal Molecular Biology and Metal Homeostasis

Investigating copper-associated metal trafficking, nutrient regulation, and cellular metal economy in photosynthetic algae through experimental and computational approaches. This work aims to better understand how algae maintain metal homeostasis and adapt to changing environmental conditions.

Systems Biology and Computational Biotechnology

Developing and applying bioinformatics pipelines, data-driven analysis methods, and AI-assisted computational tools to generate and test biological hypotheses. Current projects focus on uncovering regulatory pathways involved in metal utilization and nutrient sensing in algae.

Neurodegenerative Disease Mechanisms

Studying the molecular mechanisms underlying neurodegenerative diseases, particularly the roles of amyloid aggregation, protein misfolding, neuroinflammation, and immune-brain interactions in disorders such as Alzheimer's disease.

Computational Drug Discovery and Protein Therapeutics

Interested in the use of computational approaches for therapeutic development, including membrane protein targeting, structure prediction, molecular modeling, and rational drug design for complex diseases.

Molecular Biology & Biochemistry Techniques

Molecular Cloning, PCR, Gibson Assembly, Agarose Gel Electrophoresis, Gel Extraction, Plasmid Purification, DNA Sequencing Analysis, Bacterial Transformation & Culture, Recombinant Protein Expression, Protein Purification (Affinity Chromatography/IMAC), SDS-PAGE, Coomassie Staining, BCA Protein Assay, DNA & Protein Quantification, Spectrophotometry, Fluorescence Assays.

Chemical & Analytical Techniques

HPLC, GC, Ion Exchange Chromatography, 1H & 13C NMR Spectroscopy, Absorbance & Fluorescence Spectroscopy, Flash Kinetics Spectroscopy, MP-AES, Potentiometry, Liquid-Liquid Extraction, Column Chromatography, Recrystallization, Organic Synthesis, Polymer Synthesis & Recycling.
