About me

I am a student-athlete at the University of California, Berkeley, pursuing a Bachelor of Science in Chemical Engineering with a concentration in Biotechnology and a minor in Data Science. I was born in London, United Kingdom, and later moved to Prague, Czech Republic. I have embraced the challenges of adapting to new academic and cultural environments, both in my studies and as a member of the Cal Ice Hockey Team. Balancing athletics and academics has taught me useful time management, teamwork, hard work, and diligence skills. My academic interests are centered around the intersection of engineering and innovation.

My academic and research passions focus on chemical engineering and more specifically biotechnology, materials science, and sustainable technology. I am especially interested by process engineering’s role in improving the efficiency of systems. I am currently researching computational drug development at the Singapore University of Technology and Design. I am proficient in programming in Python, MATLAB, and Java. I speak English and Czech fluently as well as German at a B2 level. I work well in collaborative environments.

Experience

  1. Computational Drug Development Research at Singapore University of Technology & Design

    June 2025 - Present

    As an Undergraduate Researcher under Professor Desmond Loke at SUTD, I prepared and delivered presentations reviewing cutting edge literature on computational drug development for membrane protein targets, molecular pathology of neurodegenerative diseases by cryo-electron microscopy of amyloids, and hierarchical chemical determination of amyloid polymorphs in neurodegenerative disease, which prompted thoughtful discussions within the research group. I am currently working on computational research to advance drug design through structure prediction, in silico screening, and synthesis planning. I am also co-authoring a research publication on amyloid fibrils, which focuses on several key areas including investigating the effect of physical state changes on amyloid formation, analysing how chemical modifications alter amyloid structure and formation kinetics, and elucidating the high-resolution atomic structures of amyloid fibrils using cryo-EM. This research paper is currently in the initial draft phase, and is expected to be finalised in the coming months. I am also a member of another research group working on a manuscript titled Ultraefficient thermal ablation and electrothermal cancer therapy using polyethylene glycol (PEG-) based niobium carbide MXene nanomaterials. This manuscript is in its final editing phase and will be submitted for publication shortly.
